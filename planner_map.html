The cage rules (our code, never the LLM)

Every plan must pass:
  • 1 to 8 steps, never more
  • lane must be a REAL engine (numbers / text)
  • waits-for may only point to an EARLIER step
  • unreadable reply → fallback = both lanes, one wave (a bad plan can never do worse than no plan)
  • step questions in PLAIN BUSINESS LANGUAGE — the planner decides WHAT each step asks, never HOW (no sheet/column/filter names; each engine picks those from its own catalog). Lesson found in today's smoke test: pseudo-SQL wording steered both engines wrong.
Two RBAC fences:
  • Fence 1 ✅ (built): the catalog handed to the planner is pre-filtered per user by our script
  • Fence 2 (with multi-user later): the legs themselves filter at retrieval — a poisoned plan hits a locked door

Depth = number of waves (time) · Breadth = steps per wave (parallel work) · Cost = total steps.
